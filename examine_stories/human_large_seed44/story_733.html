Story with Coreference Chains

Jennifer cautiously approached Gary as he sat behind a large desk . She knew how dangerous and capricious he can be . On the desk the ledger sat , opened to the transactions in question . Large candles illuminated the room .

Gary has seen better days . A turf war with a new organization has left him on edge . His empire is weakening . An extension of himself , he feels his own vitality waning . He says , `` Can you explain why we 've been losing money in our southern district ? ''

Jennifer knew he would ask about the lost business . She had come prepared to answer his questions , perhaps allow him to understand the new reality of the situation , or shoot him in the heart if necessary . She was a bit shocked at how he looked . She said , `` Are you not well ? ''

Jennifer stood still , one hand touching the table , as Gary looked down without speaking . He had a way of disarming people , simultaneously putting the fear in them while earning their respect , trust , and sometimes their love . He was a brutal man who gave nor asked for quarter .

Gary looked now at the ledger . He still did not speak . Jennifer refused to break the silence . She waited and watched .

Finally Gary looked up , more lines on his face than when Jennifer had seen him a week ago . He stared at her for what seemed an eternity . He saw the qualities he identified when she was ten . Still there , burning brighter than ever . He knew she was ready for what came next . He said , `` I think it is time we discuss my succession plan . ''
