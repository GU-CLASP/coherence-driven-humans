She hung the sheets in the sunshine , listening to the birdsong of morning , the breeze ruffling her hair . She could hear the bang and crack of her husband splitting wood .

He swung the axe high and slammed it down on the logs , one at a time , each splitting expertly into pieces that would fit in their wood stove . He was in the rhythm of it , and it was satisfying work .

She was heading back into their farmhouse when she heard the squawking of a chicken , and turned to see her husband beheading it with the axe . It was going to be a good supper tonight , for a change .

He called out to his wife , asking her to fetch water from the well .

She ran to the well , knowing full well if she was not quick that he would punish her .

She filled the bucket at the well .

He looked down at the headless chicken and pondered the situation . Living in this remote outpost was hard , he wondered if and when life would get easier .

She ran back to him , water sloshing out of the pan , her bare feet padding against the cool dewy grass . [ SENT ]
