An entrance sign for a diner makes sure people know where to go by including an arrow , as well as text telling people to use the front door .

Betty and Rita follow the instructions and head toward the diner .

Rita stops and hesitates going in . She has been depressed lately , and she really does n't want to watch Betty try to cheer her up . She also does n't want to deal with a crowd of people , not to mention the fact that she has n't had much of an appetite lately and the greasy diner food is n't likely to change that .

Rita tells Betty this was a well-intentioned bad idea , but she 'd rather go back home . Betty reminds Rita that she has been just sitting in her house for weeks , which is n't helping the depression . This is the first day in quite a while that Rita has made herself presentable and gone out in public , and Betty can see this has already brightened up Rita 's mood a little . Rita still hesitates , but Betty promises her all they will do is sit , and sitting at the diner or sitting at home amounts to the same thing , and they are already here , so why not just go in .

Since Rita clearly does n't want to talk about her depression or problems she 's having , Betty picks up a newspaper and skims it , letting Rita know that if Rita wants to talk , she 's available , but if not , that 's fine too . Rita relaxes and gets lost in thought .
