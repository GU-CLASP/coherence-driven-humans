She was blind . Blind since birth , in fact . But she knew the world around her through sound and touch . Touch like the soft cotton of a sheet hanging on a clothesline , drying in the wind .

She also knew the sound of chopping wood , for she 'd heard it nearly every day for her twenty-five years . She could even tell who was doing the chopping based upon their strength and rhythm . In this case , it was Willem .

She had n't talked to him Willem since the night he tried to put her hand on his warm spot without asking . It was wrong for her brother to do that and he knew it . She screamed , or at least she thought she did , and ran off into the fields .

Willem felt terrible about this but he did n't see how they could fix what happened . The damage had been done . He kept his distance .

He watched as she sprinted from the house to the well to get water . There was no clear reason for her rush . It could only be that she did not want to be caught by Willem again . His heart sank .

He could see her there , stooped over . He wanted to go to her but resisted .

It was time for him to leave his home . He knew it . She knew it . The wind knew it .

Would he even tell her ?
